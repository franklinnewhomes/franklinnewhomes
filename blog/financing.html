How to Finance a New Construction Home

Understand your options and maximize savings.

Financing a new construction home is slightly different than purchasing an existing property. Understanding the process upfront can save you time, stress, and money.

Mortgage Pre-Approval

Most builders require pre-approval before entering a contract. This helps lock in your budget and strengthens your negotiating position.

Construction-to-Permanent Loans

Some buyers use construction loans that convert into a traditional mortgage once the home is completed. These loans simplify financing but require careful planning.

Builder Incentives

Many builders offer incentives such as closing cost credits or upgrade allowances when you use their preferred lender.

Down Payments & Closing Costs

Expect to budget for down payments ranging from 5–20%, depending on loan type, along with closing costs and optional upgrades.
